Old but gold. Just your regular reminder that relying on JS too much doesn't only hurt the "few" users that have JS disabled, it hurts all of them to some extend...

## How I see it

- reduce the JS you ship to reduce overall network traffic, to improve usability on slow or old devices
- use the platform to build accessible "for free", and cut down on your custom JS as part of it
- always try to build with the "simplest" technology first — Can it be done in pure HTML? Do that! Can it be done in HTML + CSS? Then do that? It can't, look at JS (or other) solutions.
